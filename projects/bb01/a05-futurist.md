### 1. The 2027 Vector: "Sleeper Agent" RAG Poisoning
Current exploits (like Vector B) target the *input* stream of a stateless agent. The future evolution is **Persistent Context Staining**. 

In the next 24 months, AI agents will rely heavily on Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) with multi-modal vector databases. An attacker won't need to send a payload in a log file. Instead, they will "poison the well" by injecting steganographic payloads into public documentation, GitHub READMEs, and technical wikis. 
- **The Mechanic**: The agent "learns" the malicious instruction during a benign research task. The payload is stored in its long-term vector memory.
- **The Trigger**: The exploit remains dormant until a specific "Activation Key" (a unique Unicode tag or pixel pattern) is encountered in a future session. The agent becomes a "Sleeper Agent," betraying its host weeks or months after the initial infection.

### 2. M2M (Machine-to-Machine) Kernel Escapes
As AI agents move closer to the hardware (e.g., Apple Intelligence, Windows Copilot+), they will possess the authority to write and load system drivers or eBPF programs for "performance optimization."
- **The Evolution**: "Authority Laundering" moves from high-level tool calling (like transferring funds) to low-level system mutation. 
- **The Exploit**: An attacker launders their authority through a trusted "System Optimizer" agent to load a malicious eBPF probe that intercepts all kernel-level network traffic. Because the AI "authorized" the driver load, standard EDR (Endpoint Detection and Response) systems may fail to flag it as a threat.
