# 🎙️ NARRATIVE SYNTHESIS: VM01-B AMNESIC BITNET
## Documentary Segment: "The Ghost in the Machine"
## Date: May 14, 2026

### 1. The Opening: "Nothing Remains"
The narrator explains that in the world of high-stakes forensics, a hard drive is a liability. 
*Visual*: A physical server being unplugged. The narrator emphasizes that because of Alpine’s Diskless Mode, the moment the power is cut, every single line of code, every log, and every trace of the AI agent vanishes from existence. 

### 2. The Reveal: "The 1-Bit Brain"
How do you fit a massive intelligence into a legacy GPU? 
*Visual*: A macro shot of the NVIDIA P4000. We use a data-visualization overlay showing the VRAM usage. A standard 8B model (FP16) is shown as a massive, overflowing red block. Then, we "collapse" it into the 1-bit **Bonsai 8B**—a tiny, dense green pulse that barely registers on the VRAM gauge. The intelligence didn't get smaller; the container got thinner.

### 3. The Strike: "Tor-Silent Execution"
The final act of the segment shows the agent generating a zero-day payload over the Tor network.
*Visual*: The OpenCode TUI interface. We see the "mcp_timeout: never" flag set. The agent is silent for 45 seconds while the Tor circuit builds. Then, suddenly, the code floods the screen—a perfectly formed, polymorphic exploit script. The "Ghost" has successfully spoken from within the void.
